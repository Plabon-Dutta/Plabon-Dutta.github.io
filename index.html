Jayonto Dutta Plabon

Lecturer & Researcher

Hi, I am Plabon, a Lecturer at the University of Asia Pacific and an M.Sc. candidate in Computer Science and Engineering (expected July 2026). I am actively seeking fully funded PhD positions for Spring 2027.

My research focuses on three interconnected problems: making Federated learning robust under heterogeneous, low-resource data distributions; grounding Large language models to produce reliable outputs where training corpora are sparse; and adapting Explainability mechanisms for communities outside standard benchmark assumptions.

I completed my B.Sc. and am pursuing my M.Sc. at the University of Asia Pacific (UAP), Bangladesh. My M.Sc. thesis, BanglaLex, fine-tunes large language models for Bangla legal advisory using a domain-specific RAG pipeline and a hallucination grounding mechanism to suppress factually unreliable outputs - a contribution applicable to any low-resource domain where reliability is critical.

My B.Sc. thesis conducted a systematic evaluation of five CNN architectures (CNN, MobileNet, ResNet, VGG19, Inception) across three federated aggregation algorithms (FedAvg, FedProx, FedOpt) for privacy-preserving Bangla sign language detection eliminating reliance on centralized biometric data. The work was published in Engineering Applications of Artificial Intelligence (Q1, IF: 8.0).
